US Legislation

The Right to Repair movement in the USA advocates for legislation that would require manufacturers to provide access to repair information, tools, and parts to independent repair technicians and farmers. This movement is particularly relevant to farm machinery, as farmers often rely on equipment that is expensive to repair or replace and require the ability to quickly and affordably fix their equipment to continue their livelihood. Proponents argue that the right to repair would benefit farmers, promote competition and innovation, and reduce e-waste.
